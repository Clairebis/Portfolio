AGF Basketball

2nd Semester Exam Project


Task: Develop a concept and an interactive digital solution for a company or another external partner.

The problem we chose to work with in this project was "How can we improve AGF Basketballâ€™s website to encourage more potential sponsors to support the club?".

AGF Basketball, a volunteer-led association, has an ongoing discussion about club growth, which requires additional funding. We saw an opportunity for the club to boost its financial standing by redesigning its website to make it easier for potential sponsors to access information and contact the club, hopefully attracting more sponsors and bringing in more funding.

We spent the first 4 weeks on research, design, testing, and building the first version of our solution in Wordpress. We then had two extra weeks to focus on our exam, and come up with additional ideas to enhance the solution.

Process

We used Design Thinking to guide us throughout the project. It helped us keep the potential sponsors in focus and to work iteratively, making changes based on feedback as we went.

1. Empathise and Define

In this stage we got to know AGF Basketball, the target audience (potential sponsors), and defined the problem. We did this through desk and field reserach. Target audience reseach helped us discover some of the motivations potential sponsors have for sponsoring. These included:

  • Supporting the local community
  • Support something they're passionate about
  • Getting some exposure for their company

We found that lack of time to research potential sponsorship opportunities was one of the main challenges to sponsoring a team faced by local businesses.

Based on our research, we created our persona, Peter, who guided us throughout the process. We also created a Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas. We made sure to base decisions we made on Peter's user goals and the business objectives. We examined the existing solution, looked at similar solutions, and did experience mapping to understand the journey a business owner would have while exploring sponsorship opportunities at AGF Basketball.

2. Ideate and Prototype

Following a How Might We session and our analysis of the existing website, we realised we wanted to work with information architecture and redesign the layout of the site. We started tesing with Card Sorting, but quickly realised the task was too challenging for our testers. Instead, each team member worked on their own to organise the data. We then compared our results and created a new structure for the site.

We came up with our list of requirements, and did some crazy 8 sketching to generate lots of ideas. We voted on ideas to take forward, and then did detailed sketches which were a valuable reference point for our wireframes.

When working on our wireframes and prototypes, we considered UX laws, Gestalt laws, notification design. e.g. feedback, and worked with atomic design when creating our own design system for the project. We created colour styles based on our moodboard and style tile, and were then able to edit our components to reflect these.

3. Testing and Iterations

In addition to early stage Card Sorting, we did Think Aloud testing at lo-fi and hi-fi stages to get as much feedback as possible. We used feedback capture grids to gather the information and made changes to our designs based on the feedback.

Overall users liked the clear structure of the site, and found it easy to navigate. Some changes we made based on user testing include:

  • Updating the look of the cards in the "Practical" section
  • Including more visuals to make the website look less text heavy and more appealing
  • Adjusting the look of the sponsorship carousel to make it more consistent with the rest of our design.

Code - Implementing the solution

We chose the Astra theme in Wordpress for its lightweight nature, speed and flexibility. We worked with Elementor, as we felt it gave us more control over our code than using Gutenberg blocks. We enabled the Flexbox container feature to help with our layouts and enabled the post name permalink structure to make our URLs more readable and SEO friendly.

1. Customisations

One area I focused on for customising the solution was the Senior and Youth Teams Accordions. The existing solution had a long list of the teams and team information that the user had to scroll through. We wanted to create an accordion so the user could open the team they were interested in and see information that was relevant to them.

The Elementor accordion widgets didn't allow us to customise the accordion in the way we wanted to (information organised into columns, images, buttons, etc.), so I created my own accordion using an HTML block. I could then style this how I wanted using CSS. I also used JavaScript to include a for loop that added an Event Listener that runs a function on click. The function adds the .active class and toggles between showing and hiding the accordion panel.

We added custom CSS and JS through the Simple Custom CSS and JS plugin, and additional JavaScript by adding script tags in our custom HTML blocks.

2. Plugins

We used a number of plugins including:

  • Smart Slider 3 - this allowed us to create a slider hero banner for the website, showing different images with relevant taglines and CTA buttons.
  • MetForm - we used this to create various forms for the website. We couldn't add these forms to a form accordion as we had planned, so we came up with a new design using the Elementor Tabs widget to keep each form in a container within each tab.

3. Further Improvements

During the extra time we had after handing in our project before the exam, one of the areas I focused on was creating a better training schedule. The existing solution had an image uploaded of a schedule created in a spreadsheet. This wasn't accessible, and the client would like to see the information displayed in a new way.

I created a new schedule in a table format, where users could search and filter to see the information relevent to them. I used the DataTables plug-in for the jQuery JS library, and created the table in a custom HTML block.
