Smukfest

Smukfest & IBM

UX / UI Project


Task: Improve the festival experience for the attendees, by providing them with a digital way to enhance their festival experience

In this project we worked to improve a mobile app for festival goers called "Min Festival". The app had been developed by IBM in cooperation with Smukfest, and they wanted our input on how we could improve the app and its features.

Our aim was to improve the app to provide users with a better experience both while using the app and during the festival.

User Research, Client Research, Figma, WP

4 weeks, group project

Process

We used Design Thinking to guide us through the project. It helped us keep the user in focus and to work iteratively.

1. Empathise and Define

In this stage we got to know the client (Smukfest), the users, and defined the problem. We did this through desk and field reserach. User reseach helped us discover some of the problems festival goers face. These included:

  • Trouble navigating the festival zone
  • Losing and finding friends
  • Challenges gathering memories

Based on our research we created our persona, Sofie, who guided us throughout the process. We also created a Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas. We made sure to base decisions we made on Sofie's user goals and the business objectives. We also did experience mapping to understand what users would be doing, thinking and feeling as part of their festival experience.

2. Ideate and Prototype

Following a How Might We session, we did several rounds of sketching to generate lots of ideas.

Some of the sketching methods we used included:

  • Crazy 8s based on adding value to the user
  • Crazy 8s based on specific parts of the UI
  • Individual solution sketching

We voted on ideas to take forward, worked out our requirements for the solution, then did detailed sketches and a user flow.

When working on our wireframes and prototypes, we considered UX laws, notification design. e.g. feedback, and made sure to refer to the Design Guide we were provided with by Smukfest. The guide was our starting point for colours and typography, etc.

3. Testing and Iterations

We created some quick "rough" wireframes to allow us to carry out guerilla testing of various ideas we had based on our sketching sessions. We wanted to get feedback on them as soon as possible.

We also did Think Aloud testing at lo-fi and hi-fi stages to get as much feedback as possible. We used feedback capture grids to gather the information and made changes to our designs based on the feedback.

Some features users liked and we included in our app were:

  • A custom pin option to be used on the map. This helps users navigate the festival and personalise their experience.
  • A friend list with notification options to allow for communication between friends within the app.
  • A scanning option to improve safety at the festival. This allowed medical workers to scan a festival goer's wristband and reach out to a their emergency contact if needed.

Code - Implementing the solution

This was our first project using Wordpress, and we spent our time getting to know our way around the CMS and learning how to use it to create parts of our app.

1. Customisations

We worked with a mix of the blocks available in the Gutenberg block editor and custom HTML blocks where we added our own code.

We added custom CSS through the Simple Custom CSS and JS plugin, and JavaScript by adding script tags in the HTML blocks.

2. Plugins

We considered a number of plugins and used e.g. the Kadence Blocks plugin to add tabs to our events page.

We also used a Sticky Block plugin to make the sub-navigation fixed at the top when scrolling through the friends list.

3. Templates

We created a template in the editor for the header and bottom navigation bar, so we could reuse these on pages that needed them.

During coding we realised we could have made better use of the templates, but we took this as a learning experience we could use in our next project.
